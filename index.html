A read on where AI actually fits in Peli Health’s stack, and where it does not.

A one-page note. Scan in a minute, read in five, keep if it holds up.

Prepared by Matt Creamer for the Peli Health team

01 / Where you are The stack today

Peli Health launched in May 2024. Pelvic Power Hours runs twice a month with a rotating expert roster. The front end is Wix, the community and courses live on Mighty Networks, and the audience grows on LinkedIn and YouTube.

That stack is sensible at three people. It is also the stack that makes a payer or hospital pilot harder than it needs to be, because every expert hour you produce lives in a slightly different place, with a slightly different name, and no shared structure a partner could inherit.

02 / The frame Where AI earns its keep

Most of what founders hear at a conference is “add AI here.” Our read on Peli Health is almost the opposite.

  • 60%
    Traditional code and structured templates.

    The content production pipeline. Scheduling, editing, captioning, repurposing, archiving. This is workflow, not language modelling.

  • 30%
    Rule-based logic and ontology work.

    Editorial judgment. Which expert goes with which symptom, which piece belongs in postpartum versus menopause, what a hospital partner would recognise as credible.

  • 10%
    Where a language model earns its keep.

    The long tail. A member asks a question at 11pm and deserves an answer shaped by your library, not a generic wellness blog.

We would apply that frame to your specific stack before we wrote a line of code.

03 / Precedent A close parallel

We ran a scoped sprint with Feeld, working with their CTO on the same shape of problem. A product company, a small team, a working context that was in people’s heads and needed to be on disk. Deliverables were a workshop, advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework. They got something their next hire could use on week one.

Peli Health is a close enough parallel that the sprint shape ports directly. Swap Feeld’s product-ops surface for your content-and-expert-library surface, and the deliverable is the same shape.

The twice-monthly Pelvic Power Hours cycle compresses from one founder-week into roughly two working days, once each stage is a contract rather than a habit.
04 / The paper Microservice Workflow Patterns

Jake co-authored Microservice Workflow Patterns alongside our Interpretable Context Methodology work. The short version is that a recurring expert-driven workflow (Pelvic Power Hours is the canonical example) can be broken into small, independently operable stages with defined contracts, each runnable by a person or a small agent.
